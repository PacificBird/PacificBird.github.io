Structure to Community to Individual

"A review on the progress (or lack thereof) in racial equality in the United States following the 1960s"
This was my final essay for my English Composition 101 class during my first year of dual-credit college classes. It explores the decidedly poor progress and extensive backpedaling that has occurred in the area of racial justice (especially pertaining to African Americans) seen in the United States following the Civil Rights Movement of the 1960's. Topics follow from racism in the housing and loan market, to the consequent cycles of poverty, civil abandonment, and the many inequalities in schooling, healthcare, policing, and quality of life.

Basic Income, AI, and the Future of the Working Class

This was my final essay for my English Composition 102 class the quarter after I wrote the other essay for 101. There are some things that I'm proud of in this essay, I think it's very well researched and evidenced, and I think I make a lot of good points and explore a few good topics, but at the same time the constraint to talking about basic income to keep in within the context of allowed topics for the assignment lead to me having to make quite a few statist and liberal takes. Keep the fact that I don't actually believe basic income is the be all and end all in terms of liberating the working class, and that in my eyes, it only constitutes a portion of possible stopgap solutions for the ever-stubborn United States.

[WIP] The Theory of Agency

This is my work in progress essay on my theory of agency as a socio-material resource and its implications for historical analyses and political and social revolution, inspired by writers such as Abdullah Öcalan, Murray Bookchin, Noam Chomsky, as well as the people of the revolutionary movements in Rojava, the Zapatista territory, and Barbacha. Please keep in mind that these ideas are very much in their earliest stages of development. I'm in a constant state of revising all the ideas and conjectures and how they are phrased, so any ideas herein do not reflect the truest sense of my belief.
